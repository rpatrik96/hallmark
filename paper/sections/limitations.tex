\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Dataset scale and coverage.}
At 2,525 entries with $\geq$30 per type per split, per-type comparisons are meaningful (95\% CI width $\leq 0.24$; MDE $\geq 20$pp at 80\% power) but small relative to the full diversity of citation hallucinations.
Prevalence differs across splits (56.6--65.5\%), so we emphasize prevalence-independent metrics (DR, FPR).
We cover English-language BibTeX from five ML venues (2021--2023); the taxonomy and sub-tests are domain-agnostic, and ${\sim}38\%$ of valid entries lack DOIs (per-type metrics expose this residual artifact).

\paragraph{Synthetic--real gap.}
Most hallucinated entries are perturbation-generated; we also include 280 LLM-generated and 108 real-world entries.
The real-world sample is small and type-skewed (55\% \texttt{plausible\_fabrication}); 15 compound-failure-mode entries involved judgment calls without inter-annotator agreement.
Expanding real-world coverage from retraction databases is a priority.

\paragraph{Temporal fragility.}
LLM verifiers carry implicit training-cutoff bias.
A 448-entry temporal supplement (\cref{app:temporal_supplement}) confirms GPT-5.1's FPR rises 17.1\%$\to$72.6\% on 2024--2025 papers; the five non-anomalous models collapse to F1\,${\approx}0.53$ (DeepSeek-R1 to F1\,=\,0 via \texttt{UNCERTAIN} routing). Two post-freeze Anthropic models (Sonnet~4.6, Opus~4.7) avoid the collapse, suggesting the failure is fixable at the training-pipeline level---but for any verifier that has not been pretrained or post-trained for this kind of metacognitive abstention, temporal robustness remains a moving target.

\paragraph{Benchmark--tool co-design and external validity.}
The taxonomy predates bibtex-updater and reflects fundamental verification steps, not tool-specific internals; we report bibtex-updater separately (\cref{app:codesign}) and use identical pre-screening for all tools.
Whether rankings on synthetic hallucinations predict performance on real errors remains open~\citep{hardt2025benchmarks}; the 108 real-world entries are an initial signal, and the codebase tracks dev-split evaluation budget~\citep{dwork2015}.
