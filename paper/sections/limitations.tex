\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Dataset scale and coverage.}
At 2,525 entries with $\geq$30 per type per split, the dataset provides meaningful per-type comparisons (95\% CI width $\leq 0.24$; MDE $\geq 20$pp at 80\% power) but remains small relative to the full diversity of citation hallucinations.
Hallucination prevalence differs across splits (56.6--65.5\%), so we emphasize prevalence-independent metrics (DR, FPR).
The benchmark covers only English-language BibTeX references from five ML venues (2021--2023); the taxonomy and sub-tests are domain-agnostic, and practitioners can supply domain-specific entries.
Approximately 38\% of valid entries lack DOI fields; per-type metrics enable assessment of this residual artifact.

\paragraph{Synthetic--real gap.}
The majority of hallucinated entries are perturbation-generated; the benchmark also includes 280 LLM-generated and 108 real-world entries.
The real-world sample is small and type-skewed (55\% \texttt{plausible\_fabrication}), and type assignment for 15 compound-failure-mode entries involved judgment calls without inter-annotator agreement measurement.
Expanding real-world coverage from retraction databases and systematic audits is a priority.

\paragraph{Temporal fragility.}
LLM verifiers carry an implicit temporal bias: parametric knowledge has a training cutoff beyond which genuine papers become unrecognizable.
A 480-entry temporal supplement (\cref{app:temporal_supplement}) confirms that GPT-5.1's FPR rises from 17.1\% to 72.6\% on 2024--2025 papers, and all models show F1 collapsing to ${\approx}0.53$.
This is structural---not a calibration issue---and makes temporal robustness a moving target benchmarks must track continuously.

\paragraph{Benchmark--tool co-design.}
The taxonomy was designed from documented incidents before bibtex-updater was developed; the sub-tests reflect fundamental verification steps (DOI resolution, title lookup, author matching), not tool-specific internals.
We mitigate residual risk by reporting bibtex-updater separately (\cref{app:codesign}), using identical pre-screening infrastructure for all tools, and releasing the full protocol for independent replication.

\paragraph{External validity.}
Whether rankings on synthetic hallucinations predict performance on real citation errors remains open~\citep{hardt2025benchmarks}.
The 108 real-world entries provide an initial signal; a dedicated external validation set is future work.
Repeated evaluation against the dev split consumes statistical budget~\citep{dwork2015}; the codebase tracks evaluation history and reports remaining budget.
