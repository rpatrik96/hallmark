\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Citation hallucination in the LLM era.}
LLM hallucination~\citep{ji2023survey,huang2023survey} manifests distinctly in citations~\citep{alkaissi2023,agrawal2024}; audits confirm scale (\citet{ghostcite2026,hallucitation2026,mysterious2026}).
Detection tools---HaRC~\citep{harc2024}, verify-citations~\citep{verifycitations2025}, RefChecker~\citep{refchecker2024}---use different protocols, blocking comparison. RefChecker requires full-text manuscript context, outside \textsc{Hallmark}'s metadata scope.
Concurrent work~\citep{rao2026bibtex,raowong2026urls,checkifexist2026,hallucitechecker2026} evaluates LLM-as-citation-generator accuracy or releases narrow URL/existence checkers; \textsc{Hallmark} is complementary, benchmarking standalone \emph{detection} tools on a controlled, tiered taxonomy with sub-test decomposition.

\paragraph{Hallucination detection benchmarks.}
General-purpose benchmarks~\citep{hallueval2023,halogen2024} target factual claims, not metadata; adjacent work---FactScore~\citep{min2023factscore}, RARR~\citep{gao2023rarr}, SciFact~\citep{wadden2020scifact}---does not evaluate BibTeX integrity (DOI/title/author/venue cross-consistency against external databases).

\paragraph{Benchmark design principles.}
\textsc{Hallmark} synthesizes design principles from established benchmarks: multi-criteria sub-tests from HumanEval~\citep{humaneval}, temporal segmentation from SWE-bench~\citep{swebench} and LiveCodeBench~\citep{livecodebench2024}, adversarial evolution from Dynabench~\citep{kiela2021dynabench}, and atomic evaluation with Plackett-Luce ranking from ONEBench~\citep{onebench2024}.
