\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Citation hallucination in the LLM era.}
LLM hallucination~\citep{ji2023survey,huang2023survey} manifests distinctly in citation generation~\citep{alkaissi2023,agrawal2024}.
Systematic audits confirmed the scale: \citet{ghostcite2026} flagged 604 papers with hallucinated references across 56K papers; \citet{hallucitation2026} found over 100 affected papers in ACL/NAACL/EMNLP; \citet{mysterious2026} documented a sharp rise across HPC venues.
Several tools address detection---HaRC~\citep{harc2024}, verify-citations~\citep{verifycitations2025}, RefChecker~\citep{refchecker2024}---but each targets different hallucination types and uses different evaluation protocols, making comparison impossible without a shared benchmark.
RefChecker operates on manuscript-level context rather than individual BibTeX entries, requiring full-text input that falls outside \textsc{Hallmark}'s metadata-focused scope; a head-to-head comparison on overlapping capabilities is future work.

\paragraph{Hallucination detection benchmarks.}
General-purpose hallucination benchmarks~\citep{hallueval2023,halogen2024} focus on factual claims rather than bibliographic metadata.
Adjacent benchmarks address related problems: FactScore~\citep{min2023factscore} evaluates atomic factual precision, RARR~\citep{gao2023rarr} targets citation revision, and SciFact~\citep{wadden2020scifact} verifies claims against abstracts.
None evaluate BibTeX metadata integrity---the structured verification of whether a cited paper's DOI, title, authors, and venue are internally consistent and match external databases.

\paragraph{Benchmark design principles.}
\textsc{Hallmark} synthesizes design principles from established benchmarks: multi-criteria sub-tests from HumanEval~\citep{humaneval}, temporal segmentation from SWE-bench~\citep{swebench} and LiveCodeBench~\citep{livecodebench2024}, adversarial evolution from Dynabench~\citep{kiela2021dynabench}, and atomic evaluation with Plackett-Luce ranking from ONEBench~\citep{onebench2024}.
